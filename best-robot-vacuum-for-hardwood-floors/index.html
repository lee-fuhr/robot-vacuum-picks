Frequently asked questions

Will a robot vacuum scratch hardwood floors?

Rubber wheels and rubber brush rolls are safer than plastic wheels and bristle brushes. The bigger scratch risk is debris trapped under the chassis being dragged across the floor, clean the robot's underside regularly.

Do robot vacuums work on dark hardwood?

Most do, but very dark floors can confuse cliff sensors, causing erratic behavior near dark rugs or transitions. Check reviews from users with dark hardwood before buying a specific model.

Can I run a robot vacuum daily on hardwood without damage?

Yes, daily light cleaning is safer for hardwood than infrequent cleaning, since debris accumulation causes more micro-scratching than the robot itself. Keep the wheels and brush roll clean.

Should I disable mopping on waxed or oiled hardwood?

Use minimal water and check your specific finish compatibility. Urethane-finished hardwood tolerates light damp mopping; antique or waxed floors need caution with any moisture.

How often should I run a robot vacuum on hardwood?

Daily in high-traffic areas, every 2-3 days in lighter-use rooms. Hardwood shows dust visibly, so frequent runs keep the appearance consistently clean.
