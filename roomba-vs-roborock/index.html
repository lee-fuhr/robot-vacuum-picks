Roomba vs Roborock comparison

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

If you value proven obstacle avoidance and a mature app ecosystem, choose Roomba. If you prefer precise lidar navigation, strong mopping, and more features for your money, Roborock is the better pick. Both brands deliver excellent cleaning; your choice comes down to mapping style and budget priorities.

How do Roomba and Roborock navigate and map your home?

Roomba uses iAdapt navigation, which relies on a camera and sensors to recognize landmarks and avoid obstacles. Earlier models can feel a bit random, but newer ones (like the s9 and j7 series) learn floor plans and offer smart mapping with room labels. The camera-based system works in low light but can struggle with black rugs or dark furniture. Roborock uses lidar (laser radar) for navigation. This creates a fast, accurate map of your home before cleaning even starts. Lidar works perfectly in total darkness and doesn’t get confused by color or reflectivity. The trade-off is a small tower on top of the robot that makes it slightly taller. Most users find lidar mapping quicker and more reliable for multi-room homes.

Which brand cleans better on carpet and hard floors?

Roomba’s rubber extractors (the dual brush roll design) are excellent at pulling dirt out of medium-pile and high-pile carpets. They are also tangle-resistant, which means less maintenance from hair wrap. On hard floors, Roomba’s suction is solid, but it relies more on the brushes to sweep debris into the path. Roborock uses a combination of a spinning side brush and a main brush (often bristle or a hybrid). Its suction power is generally very strong across the lineup, especially on models with high-suction ratings. It performs brilliantly on hard floors, picking up fine dust and larger debris with ease. On carpet, it does well at low to medium pile, but heavy carpets may need multiple passes compared to a Roomba with dual rubbers.

How does their mopping performance compare?

Roborock has a clear advantage in mopping. Most Roborock models include a water tank and a mopping pad that drags or vibrates across the floor. Higher-end versions like the S7 and newer use sonic vibration to scrub stains more effectively. The pad lifts automatically on carpets in some models, so you don’t have to remove the mop attachment. Roomba’s mopping solutions (Braava Jet or the integrated mop on the Roomba Combo j7) are more limited. The Braava Jet uses a spray-and-wipe method that works well on bare floors but can’t handle large spills or heavy grime. The Combo j7’s mop is better than nothing but doesn’t match the scrub power of a Roborock. If mopping is a priority, Roborock is the better choice.

What should you expect to pay for each brand?

Roomba spans a wide range. The entry-level models are budget-friendly and offer basic random navigation with good cleaning. Mid-range models add smart mapping and self-emptying options. The premium flagships (s9+ and j9+) come with self-emptying docks and advanced navigation, placing them in the high-end price bracket. Roborock also covers budget to premium, but you generally get more features for the price. Even mid-tier Roborock models include lidar navigation and mopping. Their top-tier models with self-emptying and auto-washing docks are similarly priced to Roomba’s premium offerings. Overall, Roborock tends to offer better value in the mid-range, while Roomba’s premium models are sometimes pricier for comparable feature sets.

How do the apps and smart features stack up?

The iRobot Home app (for Roomba) is polished and user-friendly. It lets you set schedules, define no-go zones, view cleaning history, and integrate with Alexa or Google Assistant. The app also offers smart recommendations based on your usage. One standout feature is P.O.O.P. (Pet Owner Official Promise) on the j7 series, which uses object detection to avoid pet waste and cables. Roborock’s app (Roborock, or Mi Home for older models) is equally capable. It provides a map view where you can set room divisions, schedule cleanings by room, adjust suction and water levels, and even control cleaning direction. It also integrates with voice assistants. The app’s map editing is often praised as more flexible than Roomba’s. Both apps are strong, but Roborock’s offers more granular control without an extra subscription.

Frequently asked questions

Which brand is quieter during cleaning?

Roborock models tend to be somewhat quieter on their standard cleaning mode, especially when using lower suction for hard floors. Roomba’s rubber extractors can produce more mechanical noise, particularly on carpets. That said, both have quiet and turbo options; no brand is dramatically silent.

Can Roomba and Roborock avoid pet waste and cords?

Roomba’s j7 series has the P.O.O.P. guarantee and uses a front camera to recognize and avoid pet waste, cables, and socks. Roborock’s newer models (like the S8 Pro Ultra) add obstacle avoidance via lidar and a camera, but they are less consistent with small objects. Roomba is currently the safer choice for pet owners.

Which brand has better self-emptying options?

Both offer self-emptying docks. Roomba’s Clean Base automatically empties the bin for weeks, and its bag design is straightforward. Roborock’s self-emptying docks (like the Ultra models) also wash and dry the mop pad and refill the water tank, providing a more hands-off experience. For vacuum-only, Roomba is simpler; for full automation, Roborock’s combo docks are more advanced.

Do I need a subscription for either app’s smart features?

Roomba’s iRobot Home app offers most features free, but some advanced options (like smart maps for multiple floors or the ability to name rooms) require a subscription to iRobot Select. Roborock’s app does not require any subscription for full mapping, room selection, or scheduling. Roborock is the better choice if you dislike recurring fees.

Which brand is easier to maintain and repair?

Roomba parts (brushes, filters, batteries) are widely available and easy to replace with no tools required. Many repair videos exist. Roborock parts are also available but can be trickier to source depending on your region. Roomba has a longer track record for third-party support, making it slightly easier to maintain over the long term.

Can I set up virtual boundaries or no-go zones on both?

Yes. Roomba uses physical dual-mode virtual wall barriers (sold separately) or you can set no-go zones in the app on mapping models. Roborock allows you to draw invisible walls and no-go zones directly on the map in the app. Roborock’s digital approach is more convenient and cost-effective.

In shortBoth Roomba and Roborock make excellent robot vacuums, but they serve slightly different priorities. Go with Roomba if you want the most proven obstacle avoidance and a long-support ecosystem, especially if you have pets or kids. Choose Roborock if you value precise lidar mapping, strong mopping, and a richer set of features at a competitive price. Your home’s layout and cleaning habits will ultimately decide which brand wins your living room.
