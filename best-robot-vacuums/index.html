Frequently asked questions

Is LiDAR navigation worth paying for?

Yes for most homes. LiDAR maps rooms accurately, cleans in efficient rows rather than random bouncing, and respects no-go zones precisely. The premium pays off in coverage and efficiency, particularly in larger or multi-room homes.

How often does a robot vacuum need to empty its dustbin?

Without a self-emptying dock: after every 1-3 cleans depending on home size and pet hair. With a self-emptying dock: the base bag lasts 30-60 days. Pet owners often find 2-4 weeks more realistic.

Can robot vacuums handle thick carpet?

Better ones can. Look for suction above 2,000 Pa and rubber brush rolls that resist tangling. Low-to-medium pile carpet is handled well by most quality robots; very thick rugs may still need periodic manual vacuuming.

Do robot vacuums replace a regular vacuum?

For daily maintenance, yes, scheduled daily runs keep floors consistently cleaner than weekly manual vacuuming. For deep cleaning corners and thick carpet, a periodic pass with a full-size vacuum still helps.

How do I stop a robot vacuum from getting stuck?

Lift thin rugs with fringe, use virtual barriers for cable zones, and check that floor-level lamps aren't creating traps. Dark-colored flooring near drop-offs can also confuse cliff sensors on some models.
