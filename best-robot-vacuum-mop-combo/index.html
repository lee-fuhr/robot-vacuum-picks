Frequently asked questions

Do robot vacuum mop combos actually mop well?

For light daily maintenance, dust, footprints, light grime, yes. Dried spills or stuck-on mess still need manual scrubbing. Models with sonic or oscillating pads outperform those that just drag a damp cloth.

Can a mop combo lift its pad on carpet?

Better ones do. The Roborock Q Revo and S8 Pro Ultra raise the mop pad automatically on carpet. Budget combos often don't, leaving a wet pad dragging over rugs, check before buying if you have mixed floors.

How often do I need to refill the water tank?

Most tanks last a single clean of 500-1,000 sq ft. Docks with auto-refill (like the Roborock S8 Pro Ultra) handle this automatically. Otherwise you refill before or during each run.

Do I need to clean the mop pads?

Yes, unless the dock auto-washes them. The Roborock Q Revo's dock does this; most competitors require you to remove and rinse the pad manually. Neglected pads spread grime rather than clean.

Is a combo better than two separate robots?

For most people, yes, one robot, one dock, one app. The trade-off is that combos compromise slightly on both deep vacuuming and scrub power compared to dedicated machines.
