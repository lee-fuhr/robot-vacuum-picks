Frequently asked questions

What do you give up with a budget robot vacuum?

Mostly smart navigation. Budget robots use random bump-and-go patterns rather than LiDAR mapping, so they miss spots in larger homes. You also lose self-emptying docks, precise room scheduling, and usually mop capability.

Can a $150 robot vacuum clean an entire home?

A small to medium apartment, yes, it just takes longer and misses corners more. For a large multi-room home, a budget robot will struggle to complete full coverage before its battery runs out.

Is it worth spending a little more for a smart robot?

If you can stretch to $300-400, LiDAR navigation makes a meaningful real-world difference in coverage and scheduling accuracy. The jump from $150 to $350 buys more practical improvement than $500 to $800.

Are budget robots loud?

More than premium models. The eufy RoboVac 11S is a notable exception, quieter than most at its price. Worth checking buyer feedback if noise during work hours matters to you.

Do budget robots get stuck often?

More than LiDAR models. Random navigation means repeated hits on the same obstacle. A budget robot performs much better in a clutter-free space than in a chaotic room.
