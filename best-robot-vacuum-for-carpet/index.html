Frequently asked questions

What suction power do I need for carpet?

At least 2,000 Pa for low-pile; 3,000+ Pa for medium-pile. High-pile and shag carpets are genuinely difficult, even the strongest robots struggle, and manual vacuuming remains necessary for deep cleaning.

Can robot vacuums handle carpet-to-hard-floor transitions?

Yes, for thresholds under about 0.75 inches. Most quality robots cross standard transitions without hesitation. Higher threshold strips or thick rug edges may cause the robot to stop or detour.

Do rubber or bristle brush rolls work better on carpet?

Dual rubber rolls work well on low and medium carpet and resist tangling. Bristle brushes agitate carpet more aggressively for deep cleaning but tangle badly with pet hair.

Will a robot vacuum flatten my carpet?

Not significantly. Daily light vacuuming doesn't compress carpet the way heavy furniture does.

Does boost mode help on thick carpet?

Yes, but it drains the battery faster. For very thick pile, a robot in boost mode may cover less floor area per charge. Balance coverage versus suction based on how embedded the debris typically is.
